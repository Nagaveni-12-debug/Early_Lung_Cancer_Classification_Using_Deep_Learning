Understanding Lung Cancer

Lung cancer begins when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably inside the lungs. These cells can form tumors that interfere with breathing and may spread (metastasize) to other parts of the body. There are two main types:

  • Non–Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) – Most common (85%). Includes adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and large cell carcinoma.
  • Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC) – Faster growing and usually linked to smoking.

Understanding these categories helps doctors recommend the best treatment and improves early detection accuracy.
