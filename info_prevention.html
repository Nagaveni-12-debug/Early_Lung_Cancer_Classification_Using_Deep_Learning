Prevention & Early Detection

Preventing lung cancer begins with avoiding tobacco exposure. Smoking remains the leading cause, accounting for nearly 85% of all cases. Limiting exposure to secondhand smoke, harmful chemicals, air pollution, and radon gas also plays a vital role.

Early detection uses low-dose CT scans to spot cancer in its earliest stages — often before symptoms appear. People with risk factors such as smoking history, family history, or exposure to carcinogens should undergo regular screening.
